Process

When working on projects at 3Data, I followed a straightforward and collaborative process. First, I analyzed the problem and identified the relevant stakeholders. This initial step was important to establish a game plan for the project.

Problem Analysis and Stakeholder Identification:

  • I analyzed the problem to understand its scope and impact.
  • I identified and listed all the relevant stakeholders, like customers, prospects, and internal teams, to ensure we covered all bases.

Research and Collaboration:

  • I conducted initial research, using existing company data and industry standards to gather necessary information.
  • I worked closely with my supervisor to align on project goals and strategies, which helped refine the approach and ensure it fit with the company's objectives.

Stakeholder Engagement:

  • I engaged with stakeholders through meetings, interviews, and surveys to gather their inputs. I kept them informed and involved throughout the project with continuous communication.

Informed Design Decisions:

  • I made design decisions based on comprehensive research and stakeholder inputs, making sure they aligned with both company goals and user needs.

By following these steps, I ensured my projects were well-researched, stakeholder-focused, and aligned with the company's objectives, leading to successful outcomes. This approach not only made the project execution smoother but also encouraged collaboration and utilized the strengths of various stakeholders.

Creating Business-Centric Personas

Understanding our users is crucial for designing effective solutions. I created business-centric personas to represent different user types interacting with 3Data's platform. These personas helped uncover unique needs, pain points, and motivations, guiding our design decisions to ensure we addressed diverse user requirements effectively.

Business-Centric Personas

  • The Customer: Someone who had bought into the product and sought to utilize the data visualization capabilities extensively.
  • The Ghost: Individuals who had initially engaged with the business but subsequently ceased contact, helping to identify reasons for disengagement.
  • The Trial User: Typically, a student researcher or academic interested in exploring the product without committing fully.
  • The Upfront Customer: Emerging tech enthusiasts looking to adopt new technologies quickly to solve pressing problems.
